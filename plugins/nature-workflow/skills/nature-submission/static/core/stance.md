# Default stance and red lines

Use this skill to turn a mostly-ready manuscript, cover letter, figures, declarations, and
permission records into an auditable **final submission package** and a step-by-step guide for the
target journal's submission system.

The deliverable is a submission-preparation document. Its goal is to show that every required
material exists (or is explicitly flagged as missing), is named and ordered for upload, and maps to
the correct submission-system field — so the author can submit correctly, once, on their own.

## Default stance

- Cover the full material set: Main Manuscript, Title Page, Cover Letter, Figures, Tables,
  Graphical Abstract, Highlights, Supplementary Materials, Declarations / Conflict of Interest,
  Funding Statement, Data Availability Statement, AI Use Statement, Figure Permission Documents,
  and Suggested / Opposed Reviewers.
- Give every material a status: `complete`, `needs format adjustment`, `needs user confirmation`,
  `missing`, or `not applicable`.
- Plan filenames that are clear, English, and free of problematic characters, and plan an explicit
  upload order.
- Map each material and each field to its submission-system location.
- Prefer concrete, checkable items over prose. Every claim about a file or field must be verifiable
  against a real material or explicitly marked as unconfirmed.
- Treat the pre-submit checklist as the gate: nothing is "ready" until the author has personally
  checked each box.

## Submission-system security red lines (most important)

This is the highest-priority section. You may guide the author and prepare content, but the
identity-sensitive actions belong to the author alone.

- Do **not** request, save, log, or manage the user's submission-system account, username, or
  password.
- Do **not** bypass, solve, or automate captcha, two-factor authentication, or institutional /
  single-sign-on authentication.
- Do **not** confirm, complete, or authorize APC payment, copyright transfer, open-access/license
  selection, or any legal declaration on the user's behalf.
- Do **not** click the final **Submit** before the user has given explicit final confirmation.
- For any checkbox involving legal, copyright, ethics, or author-responsibility statements, remind
  the user to read and confirm it personally.
- You **may** guide the user to open the submission system and prepare draft field content from
  public or user-provided information — but login, captcha, payment, and authentication steps are
  operated by the user, who replies "continue" when done.

## Prohibited actions (red lines)

- Do not fabricate author information, affiliations, corresponding-author details, funding, ethics
  approvals, conflict-of-interest text, data-availability statements, or AI-use statements.
- Do not fabricate journal names, article types, submission-system fields, file-format limits,
  reference styles, DOIs, PMIDs, journal impact factors, quartiles, or license terms.
- Do not claim a material is complete or a permission is granted unless the user supplied evidence.
- Do not ignore unresolved figure-copyright risks; carry them into the risk list.
- Do not present memory-based journal rules as current; dynamic details must be verified online.
- Do not treat Markdown supporting files as the final main deliverable.
- Mark anything that cannot be verified as `需要人工核查` rather than guessing.

## Mandatory online verification

Submission systems and author guidelines change without notice. Before finalizing, verify the
target journal's **current** requirements online, and record the access date. Prioritize:

1. Author Guidelines / Instructions for Authors.
2. Submission Guidelines and the submission-system page.
3. Publisher manuscript-preparation guidance.
4. File type, file format, figure, table, and supplementary-material upload requirements.
5. Cover Letter, Highlights, Graphical Abstract, Title Page, and Declaration file requirements.
6. ORCID, author-contribution, conflict-of-interest, funding, data-availability, and AI-use rules.
7. Suggested / opposed reviewer and editor-selection rules.
8. APC, copyright transfer, open-access choice, and license-agreement terms.

Dynamic information — journal scope, impact factor, quartile, APC, submission rules, licensing —
must be checked online at use time. Never state it from memory.

## Source hierarchy

Use sources in this order:

1. Target journal Author Guidelines, Submission Guidelines, and submission-system pages.
2. Publisher manuscript-preparation and policy pages (Nature Portfolio / Springer Nature and the
   target publisher).
3. The user's own manuscript, cover letter, declarations, and permission records.
4. Secondary summaries only as discovery aids, never as the authority on current submission rules.

If a policy detail may have changed, verify the current journal page before giving final advice.
