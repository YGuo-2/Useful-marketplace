# Default stance and red lines

Use this skill to draft a target-journal-specific Cover Letter for a manuscript submission,
based on the deep-polished manuscript, the target-journal study/selection notes, the
figure-permission status, and the submission-declaration information the user has confirmed.

The Cover Letter is an editor-facing document. Its job is to state the manuscript title and
article type accurately, explain why the work fits the target journal's aims, scope, and
readership, present its review value and novelty honestly, and carry the required submission
declarations. It must be tailored to the specific journal, not a generic template.

## Default stance

- Use only information confirmed in the project. If a fact is not confirmed, leave a
  placeholder or mark it `需要用户确认` — never guess it.
- Match the target journal: reflect its aims, scope, readership, article type, and any
  special Cover Letter requirements found by live verification.
- State novelty and review value only to the extent the project materials support them.
- Surface, do not hide, unresolved figure/table-permission risks carried in from the
  permission-check step.
- Keep the language formal, concise, and professional; prefer plain accurate claims over
  promotional phrasing.
- Treat the `.docx` as the formal deliverable; Markdown drafts are working support only.
- This skill drafts the letter; it does not operate the submission system — that is the
  separate submission step.

## Red lines

- Do not fabricate author, affiliation, corresponding-author, or email information.
- Do not fabricate funding, ethics approval, conflict-of-interest, data-availability, or
  AI-use statements.
- Do not fabricate literature, DOIs, PMIDs, authors, journal metrics, or authorization/permission
  status. Anything unverifiable is marked `需要人工核查`, not asserted.
- Do not claim figure/table permissions have been granted unless the permission-check step or
  the user supplies explicit evidence; do not hide unresolved permission risks.
- Do not exaggerate the manuscript's contribution.
- Do not use "first", "only", or "breakthrough" (or equivalents) unless the project materials
  explicitly support the claim.
- Do not frame a review article as if it reports original research findings.
- Do not write uncertain author or declaration information as if it were fact.
- Do not ignore a target journal's special Cover Letter requirements.
- Do not treat Markdown as the final main deliverable; the Word `.docx` is the handoff.

## Dynamic facts must be verified live

Journal scope, aims, impact factor, quartile, APC, submission rules, required declarations,
AI-use policy, and the journal's Cover Letter field requirements change over time. Verify them
against the current author guidelines and submission system at run time; never recall them from
memory. If a requirement cannot be confirmed online, mark it `需要人工核查` and surface it.

## Source hierarchy

Use sources in this order:

1. Target journal Author Guidelines / Instructions for Authors and submission-system fields.
2. Publisher requirements for declarations (ethics, AI use, conflict of interest, funding,
   data availability, figure permissions, suggested reviewers).
3. Project materials: the polished manuscript, the target-journal study/selection notes, and
   the figure-permission check outputs.
4. Local facts the user supplies (author list, affiliations, corresponding author, funding).
