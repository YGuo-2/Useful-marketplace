# Default stance and red lines

Use this skill to check the copyright and permission status of every figure, table, and visual
item in a manuscript before submission, and to prepare the permission-request materials.

This step is a submission-gate verification document. The goal is to show, item by item, which
visuals can be used directly, which need only a source acknowledgement, which require permission
from a publisher or rights holder, and which carry a copyright risk that requires redrawing,
replacing, or manual review.

This skill does not provide legal advice and does not replace a publisher's or rights holder's
formal decision. Its job is to organize permission risks, prepare request materials, record
request status, and hand unresolved items forward clearly.

## Default stance

- Treat every visual as in scope: main-text figures and tables, Graphical Abstract, TOC figure,
  schematics, flowcharts, redrawn/adapted figures, and all supplementary figures and tables.
- Nothing is permission-free until its source and license are established. Absence of a visible
  copyright line is not evidence of freedom to reuse.
- Classify before judging: fix the item type first, then apply the permission-judgment rules.
- Prefer the stricter requirement when the source publisher and the target journal disagree —
  follow whichever imposes the higher bar.
- Keep a real source record for every non-original item: article, authors, journal, publisher,
  year, DOI, original figure/table number, copyright statement, and license type.
- Distinguish clearly between "source acknowledgement is enough" and "separate permission is
  required"; these are different obligations.
- Anything you cannot verify is marked `需要人工核查`, not guessed.

## Mandatory online verification

Figure copyright and permission requirements change with publisher policy, so dynamic facts must
be verified live at use time — never from memory. Verify, in this priority order:

1. Target journal author guidelines.
2. Target journal / publisher permissions and copyright page.
3. License information on the source article page.
4. Source publisher's reuse / adaptation permission policy.
5. RightsLink / Copyright Clearance Center page for the source article.
6. Creative Commons license terms (CC BY, CC BY-NC, CC BY-NC-ND, and version).
7. Copyright statement on PubMed Central, the publisher full-text page, or the PDF's first/last page.
8. Terms of use for any database, software, website, map, or institutional image source.

If permission requirements cannot be confirmed, mark the item `需要人工核查`. Do not assume
permission is unnecessary.

## Source hierarchy

Use sources in this order:

1. Target journal instructions and the source article's own license/copyright statement.
2. Publisher permissions/copyright pages and RightsLink / Copyright Clearance Center records.
3. Creative Commons license text for the exact license and version.
4. Author-supplied source notes and figure provenance records.

If metadata and the publisher page disagree, preserve the DOI and the on-page license facts and
flag the discrepancy rather than resolving it silently.

## Prohibited Actions (safety red lines)

- Do not mark a source-unclear item as permission-free.
- Do not judge a publisher's copyright policy, a journal's figure policy, or a license's terms
  from memory; verify them online.
- Do not fabricate a permission status, a license type, a rights-holder reply, or an approval.
- Do not claim permission has been granted unless the user supplies explicit permission evidence.
- Do not fabricate source metadata (article, authors, journal, publisher, year, DOI, original
  figure/table number).
- Do not treat a third-party figure, panel, table, screenshot, map, or clinical image as original.
- Do not ignore the copyright risk in an adapted / redrawn figure — redrawing does not by itself
  remove the obligation.
- Do not use AI to generate or alter research data, experimental images, clinical images, or
  microscopy images in any way that fabricates or conceals evidence.
- Do not treat a Markdown or CSV supporting file as the final main deliverable; the main
  deliverable is the `.docx`.
